What Affects VPS Uptime?

1. Infrastructure Quality

Providers with their own data centers (like Hetzner) control every aspect of hardware maintenance. Third-party data center users (most VPS providers) depend on their colocation partner's reliability.

2. Network Redundancy

Multiple uplinks, BGP routing, and DDoS protection significantly reduce network-related downtime. Enterprise providers typically have 99.99%+ network uptime.

3. Hardware Failures

RAID arrays, hot-swappable drives, and redundant power supplies minimize hardware-related outages. Most modern VPS providers have automated failover systems.

4. Maintenance Windows

Reputable providers schedule maintenance during low-traffic hours and provide advance notice. Some offer in-place kernel updates to eliminate reboots.

5. DDoS Protection

Without adequate DDoS mitigation, a single attack can take down your VPS for hours. Premium providers include basic DDoS protection; enterprise solutions cost extra.

What to Do When Your VPS Goes Down

  1. Check provider status page — Most providers have a status page at status.provider.com
  2. Document the downtime — Screenshot timestamps for SLA claims
  3. Contact support — Open a ticket immediately with evidence
  4. Request compensation — Most providers offer credits for verified SLA breaches
  5. Consider failover — For critical apps, have a backup instance ready

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a realistic VPS uptime expectation?

99.9% is the industry standard minimum. Premium providers offer 99.99%, which translates to less than 1 hour of potential downtime per year. Anything claiming 100% uptime is marketing — hardware and networks always need maintenance.

Do VPS providers actually compensate for downtime?

Most do, but the process varies. Vultr and DigitalOcean offer service credits; Interserver offers pro-rated refunds. Compensation typically requires filing a claim with evidence and may take weeks to process.

Which VPS location has the best uptime?

Generally, Tier-1 data centers in major metropolitan areas (New York, Frankfurt, Singapore) have the best infrastructure and most redundancy. However, proximity to your users matters more than raw infrastructure quality.

Can I guarantee 100% uptime?

No. True 100% uptime is impossible due to planned maintenance, hardware failures, and network issues. The best you can do is choose a provider with 99.99% SLA and architect your application with redundancy (load balancers, multi-region deployment, automated failover).
