What is DigitalOcean?

DigitalOcean is a cloud infrastructure provider founded in 2012, specializing in simple, developer-friendly VPS hosting. Their product, called Droplets, are SSD-based virtual machines available starting at $4/month. Unlike AWS or Google Cloud with their steep learning curves, DigitalOcean built its reputation on simplicity — one-click installs, clear documentation, and a marketplace full of pre-configured apps.

After 12 years, DigitalOcean now serves over 600,000 customers and has expanded beyond just Droplets to include managed databases, Kubernetes, and App Platform. But the core question remains: is DigitalOcean still the best choice for developers and small projects in 2026?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DigitalOcean good for beginners?

Yes — DigitalOcean is arguably the best VPS for beginners. The control panel is intuitive, documentation is excellent, and the community is huge. If you're new to Linux or cloud hosting, DigitalOcean reduces the learning curve significantly.

DigitalOcean vs Vultr — which is better?

It depends on your priorities. Vultr wins on price and performance — their High Frequency plans with NVMe are faster and cheaper than DigitalOcean's Basic Droplets. DigitalOcean wins on documentation and community — better for learning and finding help. See our full Vultr vs DigitalOcean comparison.

Can I run Windows on DigitalOcean?

No, DigitalOcean doesn't offer Windows VPS. If you need Windows, consider Interserver which includes Windows VPS options on all plans.

Does DigitalOcean offer free trial?

DigitalOcean offers $200 in free credits for new accounts, valid for 60 days. This is one of the most generous trial offers in the industry — enough to run a small server for 2 months for free.

What's the difference between Basic and General Purpose Droplets?

Basic Droplets use regular SSDs and are suitable for most projects. General Purpose Droplets use NVMe storage and offer better performance for disk-intensive workloads. General Purpose plans start at $36/month vs Basic at $12/month.

Our Verdict

DigitalOcean remains an excellent choice in 2026, particularly for beginners and developers who value learning resources and community support. The $200 free credit for new users is generous, and the documentation alone makes it worth starting here.

However, if pure performance per dollar is your priority, Vultr offers better value with NVMe storage at lower prices. Think of it this way: start with DigitalOcean to learn, graduate to Vultr when you know what you're doing.
